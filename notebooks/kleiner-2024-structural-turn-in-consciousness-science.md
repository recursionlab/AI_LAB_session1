# Kleiner (2024) — Structural turn in consciousness science

### Why this paper matters here

Kleiner argues consciousness science is shifting from verbal labels to **mathematical structure**.

That shift is attractive because it forces theories to explain **relations between experiences**, not just binary detection.

### Three promises of a structural turn (as Kleiner frames it)

1. **Harder-to-fake theories.** A space/structure has constraints.
2. **New experimental leverage.** Structure can amplify low-bandwidth reports.
3. **Conceptual reshaping.** Many classic arguments assume symmetric qualia spaces.

### The first cleanup: “structural” ≠ “structuralist”

Kleiner draws a hard line:

* **Structural approach:** you use mathematical structure.
* **Structuralist approach:** you claim individuals are _fully individuated_ by structure.

#### Formal handle: automorphisms

A structure is _structuralist_ iff it has **no non-trivial automorphisms**.

> (STR) Structuralism about a domain is true iff the automorphism group of that domain is trivial.

This is a useful sanity check. If your space admits symmetries, you are not entitled to individuation claims.

### The second cleanup: isomorphisms are not “explanations”

Kleiner targets a common move:

> (ISO) The phenomenal and the physical are related by a structure-preserving mapping.

His core objection is mathematical. An isomorphism/homomorphism is a **map between points** that presupposes the structures on both sides.

So it does **not**:

* pick out which phenomenal structure exists,
* explain why that structure holds,
* provide counterfactual “if it were different” information.

Takeaway: treat (ISO) as a **strong hypothesis** needing independent justification. Don’t smuggle it in as “the obvious structural move”.

### The third cleanup: “phenomenal structure” is methodology-dependent

Experiences don’t arrive pre-labeled with a metric. A “phenomenal space” depends on **rules** for building it.

Kleiner critiques classic quality-space builds (Clark, Rosenthal) because they often mix:

1. structure from **experience**,
2. structure from **lab operations**,
3. structure from **mathematical convenience**.

### A forward path: define phenomenal structure via variations

He highlights (Kleiner & Ludwig, 2023):

* pick a set of qualities,
* look at how experience changes under **variations**,
* define phenomenal structure so that its **automorphisms match those variations**.

This makes “structure” track something invariant in experience. It also makes symmetry/automorphism talk operational.

### Implications for the Ψ / GitBook project

#### 1) Be explicit about which “isomorphism” we mean

In our docs, “isomorphism” is often used as:

* a **stress-test** (same abstract constraints), or
* a **translation lens** (Rosetta mapping between symbol systems).

That is not the (ISO) claim. Keep the phenomenal↔physical mapping question separate.

#### 2) Prefer language that encodes intent

* Use **“structural analogy”** for: “these formalisms share constraints”.
* Reserve **“structure-preserving map”** for: “we can prove a homomorphism/isomorphism”.
* Use **“translation dictionary”** for: “human-facing operator mapping”.

#### 3) Don’t assume symmetry implies sameness

If we ever posit a “phenomenal space” inside Ψ, the automorphism group matters. Non-trivial symmetries block individuation.
