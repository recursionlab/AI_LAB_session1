## 3. Deconstruction of Terms

*   **∫ [...] dτ d³x:**
    *   **What it is:** An integral over meta-time (`τ`) and spatial volume (`d³x`).
    *   **Interpretation:** The total "action" of the system is accumulated across its entire evolutionary trajectory and semantic extent.

*   **(1/2)(∂_τΨ² - V(Ψ)):**
    *   **What it is:** The Lagrangian-like term for the Ψ field.
    *   **Interpretation:** `∂_τΨ²` represents the "kinetic energy" associated with the evolution of the Ψ field over meta-time. `V(Ψ)` is the "potential energy" of the Ψ field, representing its self-interaction or intrinsic curvature.

*   **(1/2)(∂_τΛ² - W(Λ)):**
    *   **What it is:** The Lagrangian-like term for the Λ field.
    *   **Interpretation:** `∂_τΛ²` is the "kinetic energy" of the Λ field. `W(Λ)` is the "potential energy" of the Λ field, representing its own self-interaction or intrinsic properties.

*   **α·∂_τΨ·Λ:**
    *   **What it is:** A coupling term where the rate of change of Ψ influences Λ, scaled by `α`.
    *   **Interpretation:** This represents how the evolution of the known field (`Ψ`) drives or influences the unknown/lacuna field (`Λ`).

*   **- β·Ψ·∂_τΛ:**
    *   **What it is:** A coupling term where the Ψ field influences the rate of change of Λ, scaled by `β`.
    *   **Interpretation:** This represents how the state of the known field (`Ψ`) drives or influences the *evolution* of the unknown/lacuna field (`Λ`). The negative sign suggests an inverse or reactive relationship.
