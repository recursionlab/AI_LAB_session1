## 3. Deconstruction of Terms

*   **C (Collapse Operator):**
    *   **Derivation:** `C = [Meta, non-]` (negated self-reference).
    *   **Interpretation:** Represents the process of "compression-with-residue." It is the act of collapsing a superposition of possibilities or a complex thought into a single, definite state. This process is not perfect and leaves a "residue" or remainder. It is the operator of decision, distinction, and concretization.

*   **E (Expansion Operator):**
    *   **Derivation:** `E = [Ana, Pro]` (forward abstraction).
    *   **Interpretation:** Represents the process of "exploration" or "explication." It is the act of taking a concept and unfolding its potential, exploring its implications, and abstracting it into the future. It is the operator of imagination, possibility, and inquiry.

*   **[C, E]:**
    *   **What it is:** The commutator of Collapse and Expansion. It measures the degree to which these two operations fail to be interchangeable. `[C, E] = CE - EC`.
    *   **Interpretation:** The non-zero result signifies a fundamental uncertainty and non-commutativity at the heart of cognition. You cannot collapse a possibility (C) and then explore its potential (E) and get the same result as exploring its potential (E) and then collapsing it (C). The order of operations fundamentally changes the outcome.

*   **iℏ_meta (Cognitive Planck Constant):**
    *   **What it is:** A complex number representing the minimal, irreducible quantum of cognitive action.
    *   **Interpretation:** `ℏ_meta` is the fundamental "pixel" of thought. It is the smallest possible amount of "surprise," "residue," or "information" that is generated by the act of thinking (the C-E cycle). The imaginary unit `i` suggests a phase or rotational component, indicating that this process is not just about magnitude but also about a change in the "direction" or "angle" of thought in a cognitive state space.

    *   A state of infinite, open-ended exploration (low `Var(E)`) necessarily implies a state of near-zero concrete definition (high `Var(C)`).
